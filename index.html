Idea

New York Layover

The easiest way to get to the UK may be connecting through JFK. Depending on flight schedules, we could fly into New York a day early, play a round of golf, have a great dinner, and then catch our flight to Edinburgh the following day.

Idea

Kent Coast Extension

Another option: extend the trip by a few days after Scotland and head south to England's Kent coast. Three legendary links, all on neighboring property, make for an easy add-on if there's interest.

Royal St George's

One of England's most iconic links courses and a 15-time Open Championship host. Champions here include Darren Clarke (2011), Ben Curtis (2003), Greg Norman (1993), Sandy Lyle (1985), and Bobby Locke (1949). Consistently ranked among the best courses in the world.

Prince's Golf Club

Host of the 1932 Open Championship, won by Gene Sarazen, and recently transformed into one of England's premier modern links experiences.

Organizer's note: I'm playing here in September to do some reconnaissance 😁

Royal Cinque Ports

One of England's oldest and most revered links courses. Hosted The Open in 1909 (won by J.H. Taylor) and 1920 (won by George Duncan) — still one of the finest championship links in England.

Head back to London for another nice dinner before heading home.
